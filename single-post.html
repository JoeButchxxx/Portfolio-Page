Behind The Flight

Joseph Butcher / 15 April 2021 / Tech

So just how beneficial are drones moving 1/3 of the way into 2021? Battling covid many companies are adapting, being granted air carrier certificates. Drones are now carrying more weight than ever, which opens the idea to drop of contactless vaccination and medication deliveries. During the CES 2020 it was disussed how a study collecting data to prevent world disasters has been working with drones. They talked about how it has offered better aerial footage surveying entire cities, enabling the development of risk assessments at accessible costs providing better success.

Something that I think is exceptionally cool that drones are doing is catching poachers in the oceans.
The Sea Shepard Conservation Society are an international ocean conservation movement, protecting our oceans, defending wildlife, and conserving and protecting the worldâ€™s oceans from illegal exploitation and environmental destruction.

These all offer a huge advantage to our society, increasing efficiency and lowering costs. But as new jobs for drones arrise, the underlining aspect of safety continues. The dangers of crashing and costs of pilot training prevents drones from reaching their full potential.

After doing some research it seems all drones are about is safety, with sensors and Geofencing that are continuously updating to new software, the only concern is the people and that they arent suffuciently regulated. Hijacking causes concern, if someone is hacking into the drones communication system they could hack the encryption and control the device. Drones carrying small Raspberry Pi computers can be programmed to sniff for wifi signal, testing security of near by critical infrastructures is a concern. Controlled attack simulations are used to test and monitor security, in remote areas drones are used to do fly overs searching for wifi signals and hacking into the communication for research and improvement.

We are still in the early days, with a long way to go. But huge changes can happen in a short time and its good to be aware of the threats this could deliver. I am 100% for the future and how drones could take over, but regulations and safety needs to be monitored. Bringing such diverse strengths to the table, drones cant go unnoticed in 2021. The future is here and we have to embrace it.
